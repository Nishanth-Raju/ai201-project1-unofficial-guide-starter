--- COMMENTS ---

2011 is 15 years old at this point, you would need to find the 2.5L unbearably slow to make it worth it given the price is basically the same

Stereos can be updated, and it's especially easy on older cars. The 3.6 is fantastic, but I'd keep in mind that while you're avoiding car payments, you'll have maintenance and repairs. I personally prefer paying for maintenance and repairs over a car payment, but I know a lot of other people do not feel the same way. 

The 3.6r went up through 2018. If it were me, I'd take short term car payments on something newer to add a little more long term reliability. Subarus are safe, but they get safer each year, too. If safety is one of your main points, I'd recommend getting as new as possible.

That being said, what kills Subarus is lack of maintenance. A neglected 2018 won't be as reliable as a well maintained 2011, for example. Get whatever you're considering inspected by a good mechanic and be sure to follow the factory maintenance schedule that makes the most sense for you (far more people should be using the severe schedule than they realize).

Unless its the Harmon Kardon radio (top trim), the radios on the 2011 are stupid easy to replace. They take normal double DIN radios with a simple kit and you could add Carplay trivially. I wouldn't consider that a deal breaker. You release the clips for the grey center trim, and 4 screws and the radio is out. It's that simple.

The other point in the direction of the 2011 is that the 3.6 and 5EAT transmission combo are one of the best combinations Subaru made for reliability. Not that it will never have problems, but they are not known to have common issues that will leave you on the side of the road. The only downside to this is that it's going to be a bit more gas hungry. Both because of the 3.6 and 5 speed.

Other than that, most of its downsides are just going to be related to age. Not as many features, safety is going to be bit more dated, some things like rubber become worse with age, not necessarily mileage.

3.6 is the motor you want
