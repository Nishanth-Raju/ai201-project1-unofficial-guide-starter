Camrys
 produced after the change are frequently referred to as "2014.5" 
Camrys
. I'd check the production date on the 2014 
Camry
 you listed and see if it was produced before or after Toyota made those safety improvements. Apparently all 2014

 produced starting in January 2014 have the improved front structure that performs better in a crash. Some December 2013 
Camrys
 had the improvements- but it maybe tough to figure out which ones had the change or not.

. All 2015+ Camrys have the better front-end structure too.

miles- but if you can find one that has a decent maintenance history- it

cheaper to insure too. 

I'd stay away from the Mazda 3. I know too many people that have had 
bad experiences with those cars. Some years are better than others but 
I'd never pay money for one.

Of course the car industry is 
dynamic, thus always changing but I'll give you my perspective. I've 
previously owned a brand new 1992 Accord 5sp manual and my wife 
currently drives a 97 Civic. I would like to get her into a Toyota. In 
2004 I was going to a dealer to look at a 5th gen Accord A/T to replace 
our 4th gen Accord M/T. When I saw the 4th gen 
Camry

 w/Auto transmission next to the Accord, I was very impressed and it 
felt like a luxury car compared to the 5th gen accord. I bought the 
Camry
 and never looked back. It still runs and drives almost like new with over 200k miles. 

I think that historically Honda has always seemed to add a little sporty
 flavor to most of their cars. Toyota on the other hand was more 
compartmentalized. You could buy a sporty supra or a practical 
Corolla
 or 
Camry
.
 I would say that Toyota has added more sports flare lately and wonder 
if they've gone too far really for my tastes. I've driven manual 
transmissions most of my life but for the last decade have been driving 
Toyota A/Ts and really enjoying the convenience and ride quality. To 
boil it down, I think Honda's are generally more sporty and stylish (but
 this is subjective and Toyota is closing the gap), might have a little 
sportier handling, but IMO Toyota has better reliability, better than 
any car maker. Honda may be a close 2nd but according to recent data, 
Honda reliability has fallen off somewhat over the last few years. Part 
of the reason again IMO, is Honda is less conservative than Toyota or 
most Japanese makers. They tend to push the envelope more and sometimes 
reliability suffers as a result. When my wife's Civic needed a new 
tranmission with only 130k miles, I also discovered that Honda's A/Ts 
are not as reliable as Toyota's. Toyota practically makes their own 
A/Ts. Engines and transmissions are typically very expensive to repair 
and with a Honda, regular maintenance might be even more important to 
prevent a costly breakdown.

Sometimes government regulations also play a role. Do you notice many 
makers going to tiny turbo engines lately? They have to meet govt fuel 
economy goals and are going with these little over worked engines to 
meet requirements. AFAIK, you can't buy a 6 cyl Accord any longer but 
you can buy a 6 cyl 
Camry
.
 Honda had some oil dilution issues when combining turbo and GDI 
technologies. When Toyota used GDI, they also used conventional FI to 
avoid carbon buildup. The new 
Camrys
 come either with a 2.5 or 3.5 engine. I don't know how long Toyota will resist the current trend.

As far as 
Camry
 vs 
Corolla
,
 IMO same high reliability in different size packages. How much do you 
want to spend? You sound concerned over high prices so go with a 
Corolla
 which should be cheaper than a 
Camry
. If not look elsewhere or go with a 
Camry
. Toyota and Honda resale value should be about the same.
